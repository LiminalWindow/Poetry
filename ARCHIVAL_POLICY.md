# Archival Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this repository is the long-term preservation, documentation, and accessibility of the poetic works of Jaiden Hordosillo.

This repository functions as a literary archive rather than a promotional platform.

Its primary objective is to maximize the likelihood that these works remain discoverable, identifiable, and accessible to future readers, researchers, libraries, archives, and cultural institutions.

---

## Preservation Philosophy

Literature often survives not because it was commercially successful, but because it was preserved.

This repository operates under the belief that contemporary poetry deserves the same preservation consideration commonly afforded to scholarly, historical, and cultural materials.

The goal is not merely publication.

The goal is persistence.

---

## Repository Contents

Each work may include:

- Print editions
- Ebook editions
- Audio performances
- Cover artwork
- Metadata records
- Identifier records
- Cryptographic timestamps
- 3D representations
- Supplemental publication materials

Not every work will contain every format.

Formats may expand over time as preservation technologies evolve.

---

## Metadata Strategy

Whenever possible, works are associated with recognized identifier systems including:

- ISBN
- DOI
- ORCID
- ISNI
- Wikidata
- BMI
- SoundExchange
- MLC
- Library catalog records

The purpose of these identifiers is to improve long-term discoverability and establish durable links between works, creators, and institutions.

---

## Redundancy Policy

No single platform should be considered authoritative.

Works may be preserved simultaneously through:

- GitHub
- Internet Archive
- Zenodo
- Library systems
- Rights organizations
- Creator identifier systems
- Personal websites

Redundant preservation reduces the risk of loss through technological, commercial, or institutional change.

---

## Authenticity

When available, cryptographic hashes and timestamp records may accompany repository materials.

These records are provided to assist future verification efforts and establish historical provenance.

---

## Future Access

This repository is maintained with the expectation that future readers may encounter these works decades or centuries after their creation.

All preservation decisions should favor long-term accessibility, documentation, and cultural continuity.
